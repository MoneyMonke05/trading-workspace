Options Market Overview

A real-time news feed covering market-moving events and analysis.

Unusual Stock Options Activity

Identifies contracts with volume significantly higher than open interest, signaling potential large moves. Bullish sentiment is highlighted in blue, bearish in red.

Options Flow

Highlights large option trades across all U.S. exchanges, providing insights into institutional sentiment and volatility expectations.

Upcoming Earnings

A list of optionable stocks with earnings reports scheduled for the next trading day.

IV Rank & IV Percentile

IV Rank shows current Implied Volatility relative to its 1-year high/low. IV Percentile shows the percentage of days it's been lower than the current level. High IV suggests expectations of large price swings.

Implied vs Realized Volatility

Compares forward-looking implied volatility to backward-looking historical volatility. A ratio > 1.0 implies high premiums (good for sellers), while < 1.0 implies low premiums (good for buyers).

Highest Implied Volatility

Highlights strikes with elevated implied volatility, where the market anticipates a large price swing. Preferred for strategies like covered calls or credit spreads to capitalize on inflated premiums.

% Change in Volatility

A large increase in IV highlights strikes where the market anticipates a large price move, making premiums more expensive. A large decrease suggests stable prices and cheaper premiums.

Most Active Options

Ranks stocks, ETFs, and indices by their total option trading volume for the day. A high put/call volume ratio reflects bearish sentiment, while a low ratio reflects bullish sentiment.

Unusual Options Volume

Shows the difference between the current day's volume and the average volume over the past month, highlighting unusual trading activity.

Highest Open Positions

Ranks stocks, ETFs, and indices by total open interest. A high put/call open interest ratio reflects bearish sentiment, while a low ratio reflects bullish sentiment. Updated daily at market open.

% Change in Open Interest

Highlights contracts with the largest daily percentage increase in Open Interest. An increase indicates new positions are being opened, while a decrease indicates positions are being closed.
